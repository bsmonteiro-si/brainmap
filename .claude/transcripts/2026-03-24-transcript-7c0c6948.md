## Assistant

Good question. It does make sense, and it maps naturally to what BrainMap already has.

**Why it fits:**

- The data model already has 11 note types (concept, question, claim, evidence, etc.) — typed canvas cards would be the visual surface for the same idea
- A "Question" card vs a "Summary" card gives you instant visual scanning — you see the structure of your thinking, not just a wall of text blocks
- It enables queries like "show me all open questions on this canvas" or "connect every claim to its evidence"

**How it could work:**

- They're still text cards under the hood (JSON Canvas `type: "text"`), but with a `brainmap:cardKind` field in the node data (custom extension, the spec allows extra fields)
- Each kind gets a visual treatment: distinct border/header color, small icon or label badge
- The toolbar "Add Card" button could offer a dropdown: Text, Summary, Question, Claim, etc.
- A card's kind could be changeable after creation (right-click → "Change kind")

**What I'd watch out for:**

- Keep the default "Text" card frictionless — don't force users to pick a kind every time
- Start with 3-4 high-value kinds (Summary, Question, Claim, Evidence) rather than exposing all 11 note types — canvas should feel lighter than the full data model
- Consider whether a typed card should be promotable to a full note (extract to `.md` file with the matching `type` frontmatter)

The key insight is that canvas is where thinking happens *before* it's structured — typed cards let users add just enough structure without leaving the freeform space. Want me to draft a plan for this?
